Dearest creature in creation

Studying English pronunciation,

I will teach you in my verse

Sounds like corpse, corps /kɔː/, horse and worse.

I will keep you, Susy, busy,

Make your head with heat grow dizzy;

Tear in eye, your dress you'll tear;

Queer, fair seer /'siːə/, hear my prayer.

Pray, console your loving poet,

Make my coat look new, dear, sew it!

Just compare heart, hear and heard,

Dies and diet, lord and word.

Sword and sward, retain and Britain

(Mind the latter how it's written).

Made has not the sound of bade,

Say-said, pay-paid, laid but plaid /plæd/.

Now I surely will not plague you

With such words as vague and ague,

But be careful how you speak,

Say: gush /gʌʃ/, bush, steak, streak /striːk/, break, bleak,

Previous, precious, fuchsia /ˈfjuːʃə/, via

Recipe /'rɛsɪpɪ/, pipe, studding-sail /ˈstʌns(ə)l/, choir;

Woven /'wəuv(ə)n/, oven /'ʌv(ə)n/, how and low,

Script, receipt, shoe, poem, toe.

This phonetic labyrinth

Gives moss /mɔs/, gross /grəus/, brook /bruk/, brooch /brəuʧ/, ninth, plinth /plɪnθ/.

Have you ever yet endeavoured

To pronounce revered /rɪ'vɪəd/ and severed,

Demon /'diːmən/, lemon /'lemən/, ghoul /guːl/, foul, soul,

Peter, petrol and patrol /pə'trəul/?

Bouquet /bu'keɪ/, wallet /'wɔlɪt/, mallet /'mælɪt/, chalet /'ʃæleɪ/.

Blood and flood are not like food,

Nor is mould like should and would.

Banquet /'bæŋkwɪt/ is not nearly parquet /'pɑːkɪ/ /'pɑːkeɪ/,

Which exactly rhymes with khaki /'kɑːkɪ/.

Discount, viscount /'vaɪkaunt/, load and broad,

Ricocheted /'rɪkəʃeɪd/ and crocheting /'krəuʃeɪɪŋ/, croquet /'krəukeɪ/?

Right! Your pronunciation's OK.

Rounded, wounded /'wuːndɪd/, grieve and sieve,

Friend and fiend, alive and live.

Is your r correct in higher?

Keats asserts it rhymes Thalia /θəˈlaɪ.ə/.

Hugh /hjuː/, but hug, and hood /hud/, but hoot /huːt/,

Buoyant, minute, but minute.
